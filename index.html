About me

Academic

I am a third-year (Part II) astrophysics undergraduate at the University of Cambridge. My main academic interests are in general relativity and cosmology. I have experience with programming in Python, and a touch of C++. I am currently taking courses in

  • Structure and evolution of stars - Max Pettini
  • Stellar dynamics and structure of galaxies - Wyn Evans and Cathie Clarke
  • Relativity - Anastasia Fialkov
  • Principles of quantum mechanics - Enrico Pajer
  • Introduction to cosmology - Martin Haenhelt
  • Statistical physics - Christopher Thomas
  • Astrophysical fluid dynamics - Avishai Gilkis
  • Topics in astrophysics - Cathie Clarke and Suhail Dhawan

In addition, I am taking two non-examinable courses in Introduction to astrophysics and Classical dynamics, lectured by Craig Mackay and David Skinner respectively.
In second year (Part IB) I took Physics A, Physics B, and Mathematics, and in first year (Part IA) I took Chemistry, Materials Science, Physics, and Mathematics B.

Extracurricular

Music

I am a self-taught guitarist. I started playing in August 2020 with a Yamaha Pacifica 112V that I got for my 18th birthday. My second, and most recently bought, is an Ibanez RGA42HPT-LBM. I mostly listen to metal music - my favourite bands are Iron Maiden, Sabaton, and Megadeth. It's hard to narrow down my top albums and songs to a concise list, but a few of my favourites are Rust in Peace by Megadeth, Dance of Death by Iron Maiden, and Images and Words by Dream Theatre. I have a couple of more unusual instruments in my possession, including a jaw harp and a harmonica.

Astronomy

My interest in astronomy only really began when I came to Cambridge in 2021. Since then, I have been trained to use the University's 12- and 16-inch refractors. My favourite objects to observe are the Orion Nebula and Albireo. I was the Chair and Speaker Liaison of the CU Astronomical Society (23/24). My main job was organising the weekly talk series hosted throughout the year.
